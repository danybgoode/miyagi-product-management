# Neighborhood Commerce Layer

## Vision

Transform the marketplace from a transactional platform into a trusted local commerce community.

The objective is to help buyers discover products, services, artisans, and merchants through neighborhood trust, local recommendations, and community visibility rather than relying exclusively on search and listings.

## Strategic Principles

- Trust is more valuable than inventory.
- Discovery is more important than search.
- Local reputation is more meaningful than ratings alone.
- Community engagement should support commerce, not distract from it.
- The platform should feel like the digital pulse of a neighborhood.

## Community Experience

The marketplace includes a neighborhood-focused activity layer that allows users to stay informed about what is happening locally.

This includes:

- Trending local products
- Recommended merchants
- Popular services
- Artisan spotlights
- Community recommendations
- Local buying requests
- Neighborhood events and markets

The experience is designed around useful local awareness rather than social networking.

## Seller Identity

Merchants and sellers are presented as local businesses and community members rather than anonymous listings.

Profiles emphasize:

- Reputation
- Consistency
- Responsiveness
- Local presence
- Community recognition
- Merchant stories and identity

The goal is to create familiarity and confidence before a transaction occurs.

## Trust & Reputation

Trust is built through community signals rather than traditional review systems alone.

Examples include:

- Frequently recommended merchants
- Repeat buyers
- Community endorsements
- Verified local presence
- Consistent participation
- Featured neighborhood contributors

The platform rewards reliability, visibility, and positive community engagement.

## Discovery Model

Discovery combines listings with local context.

Users can explore:

- What neighbors are buying
- Which merchants are gaining attention
- Emerging local brands
- Popular services nearby
- Seasonal trends
- Curated neighborhood collections

This creates ongoing engagement even when users are not actively shopping.

## Neighborhood Pulse

A dedicated community layer acts as the heartbeat of the marketplace.

It surfaces:

- Local opportunities
- Recommendations
- Requests
- Success stories
- Featured sellers
- Community highlights

The objective is to make the marketplace feel alive, relevant, and connected to real-world neighborhoods.

## Community Participation

Participation is intentionally lightweight.

Users can:

- Recommend
- Endorse
- Save
- Share
- Support local sellers
- Contribute useful local information

The platform prioritizes simple interactions over traditional social media behaviors.

## Business Outcome

By combining commerce, reputation, and local community activity, the marketplace becomes a recurring destination rather than a place users visit only when they need to buy or sell something.

This increases:

- Trust
- Retention
- Merchant visibility
- Buyer confidence
- Community participation
- Local economic activity

## Long-Term Goal

Create the leading digital destination for neighborhood commerce in Mexico, where local discovery, community trust, and economic activity converge in a single platform.

The marketplace becomes the place where people discover what is worth buying, who is worth buying from, and what is happening in their local community.
